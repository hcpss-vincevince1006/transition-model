Model for Transition in Howard County

Overview

Preparation for adult life begins as soon as a student enters an HCPSS school. This includes giving all HCPSS students the same expectations for individual growth, while providing unique transition activities and experiences to support students with disabilities.

At each level of their education, students with disabilities are supported by both their school community and parents to successfully transition into adult, independent living.

General Expectations

Elementary school

Parents will be introduced to the IEP and transition process and gain a better understanding of their student's individual needs. This builds the foundation for their future transition plan.

Middle School

Parents begin the formal transition planning process and complete a student success plan. Students will start coming up with their post-secondary goals and parents can start applying for appropriate post-secondary adult services.

High School

Formal transition planning continues at this level. Parents and the school will come up with a career pathway that is aligned with the student's interest and ability levels. Students will take advantage of either workplace learning experiences or college readiness resources.
