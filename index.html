Model for Transition in Maryland

Elementary School

What are the expectations for all students?

Awareness of Self

Takes part in activities to discover one’s talents, strengths, interests, preferences & needs for support

Skill Development

  • Social
  • Activities of daily living
  • Academic
  • Work habits and values
  • Love of learning

Understanding of Rules

Ability to follow classroom rules and guidelines set forth by teachers.

Knowledge of College and Careers

Explores what careers exist for adults who complete school, as well as understands the concept of post-graduation education.

Sense of Community

Develops an appreciation for their school and acknowledges how the school environment is there to support and nuture them.

Self-Esteem

Engages in activities and interactions both in-school and out-of-school that boosts confidence and feeling of self-worth.

What are the transition activities to support students with disabilities?

Formal Transition planning begins at this level as part of the IEP (Individualized Education Plan)

What parents should expect from schools and can do at home:

Parents

Know your rights and responsibilities before starting the IEP process.

Parents

Begin a positive personal profile and/or portfolio with your student

Parents

Learn about & apply for appropriate adult services that your student can utilize after graduation

Schools

Come up with the student's future course of study that is tailored to the student's interests and abilities

Schools

Support students’ active involvement in the IEP process. Keep the channels of communication open and responsive.

Parents

Come up with post-secondary goals for employment, education/training, and independent living. Involve your student in these decisions to understand their wants and needs.

Parents

Learn more about training and transition experiences that your student can take advantage of during high school.

What are the transition activities to support students with disabilities?

Formal Transition planning continues at this level as part of the IEP (Individualized Education Plan)

What parents should expect from schools and can do at home:

Parents

Identify specific needs for adult services & make linkages (e.g. DD, VR)

Parents

Update their positive personal profile and/or portfolio. If they don't have one yet, create it.

Parents

If student is attending collete, meet with their college's disability support services.

Schools

Address disclosure issues to parents.

Schools

Come up with a transition career pathway that matches the student's interests and ability levels.

Schools

Ensure an appropriate high school program placement & course selection based on post-graduation goals.

Schools

Inform students/families of post-graduation follow up survey, encourage them to fill it out.

Schools

Create a summary of performance for parents to understand what their student has accomplished.

Schools

Help them take advantage of work-based learning opportunities.

Schools

Encourage students to participate in an HCPSS-sponsored workplace training program. Find out more about these programs →
